Tribute to Shivaji Maharaj

Shivaji Maharaj was brave and had an immaculate personality in Indian history. Shivaji Maharaj was the warrior king and famous for his bravery, tactics and administrative skills. He always focussed on Swarajya and Maratha heritage. He was the descendant of the 96 Maratha Clans well known as 'Kshatriyas' or brave fighters.

  • Life:
    1. Born on February 19th, 1630, in the hill fort of Shivneri, near the city of Junnar in the Pune district.
    2. His parents were Shahaji Bhosale, a Maratha general who served under the Deccan Sultanates, and Jijabai, a devout and strong-willed woman.
  • Training:
    • From a young age, Shivaji was trained in military tactics and administration, showing early signs of his strategic genius.
  • Formation of the Maratha Empire:
    • In 1674, Shivaji was crowned as the Chhatrapati (Emperor) of his realm at Raigad, marking the foundation of the Maratha Empire.
  • Conquests and Campaigns:
    • Throughout his life, Shivaji engaged in numerous battles against the Mughals, the Adilshahi Sultanate of Bijapur, and the Portuguese in India, expanding his territory significantly.
  • Death:
    • Shivaji died on April 3, 1680, at the age of 50. His cause of death is attributed to fever or possibly dysentery.
  • Legacy:
    • Shivaji Maharaj's legacy is a symbol of courage, nationalism, and administrative genius. He is celebrated as a hero in India, especially in Maharashtra, where his birth anniversary is commemorated as Shivaji Jayanti.
